Minerals & Land Resources of Uganda

Unlocking Uganda's wealth for sustainable development

Overview

Uganda is endowed with a rich variety of minerals and land resources, including gold, tin, tungsten, limestone, and rare earth elements. The country's fertile soils and diverse landscapes support agriculture, forestry, and eco-tourism, while its mineral wealth offers opportunities for economic growth and industrialization. Responsible management of these resources is key to ensuring long-term prosperity and environmental sustainability.

Major Minerals in Uganda

Gold

Found in Busia, Buhweju, and Karamoja, gold is Uganda's leading mineral export.

Tin

Extensive tin deposits are found in south-western Uganda, especially in Kabale and Kisoro.

Limestone

Vital for cement production, limestone is abundant in Hima, Tororo, and Moroto.

Rare Earth Elements

Uganda has significant rare earth deposits, especially in eastern regions like Busoga.

Land Resources

Uganda's land resources include fertile soils, forests, wetlands, and water bodies. These support agriculture, livestock, and biodiversity. Sustainable land management practices are essential to prevent degradation and ensure food security for future generations.

  • Agricultural Land: Over 80% of Uganda's land is arable, supporting crops like coffee, tea, maize, and bananas.
  • Forests: Cover about 12% of the country, providing timber, fuel, and habitat for wildlife.
  • Wetlands: Crucial for water purification, flood control, and fish breeding.

Sustainable Development

The government of Uganda, in partnership with local communities and international organizations, is committed to promoting sustainable mining and land use. This includes enforcing environmental regulations, supporting community development, and investing in value addition for minerals.

Key Milestones

2003: Mining Act Enacted

Uganda passed the Mining Act to regulate mineral exploration and ensure environmental protection.

2012: Discovery of Rare Earths

Significant rare earth element deposits discovered in eastern Uganda, opening new economic opportunities.

2020: National Land Policy Review

Uganda reviewed its land policy to strengthen land rights and promote sustainable land use.
